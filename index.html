José de San Martín

"When the homeland is in danger, everything is permitted, except not defending it."

A timeline of San Martín life:

    1778
    On February 25, José Francisco de San Martín was born in Yapeyú, the youngest of five siblings of the marriage of Don Juan de San Martín and Gregoria Matorras.
    1783
    The San Martín family moves to Spain.
    1789
    José de San Martín joins the Infantry Regiment of Murcia, Spain, as a cadet.
    1791
    He participates for the first time in combat, in Oran North Africa. Slave rebellion in Haiti.
    1804
    San Martín stands out for his bravery and courage in Cádiz.
    1810
    San Martin learns that the May Revolution has taken place in his homeland and that there is a new government that proposes profound changes and decides to return to his homeland.
    1811
    San Martin requests and obtains the withdrawal of the Spanish army. He leaves for London and from there embarks to Buenos Aires.
    1812
    Creates the Regiment of Grenadiers on Horseback. Founded with Carlos María de Alvear the Logia Lautaro, a secret organization that aimed to fight for the freedom of America. Marries Remedios de Escalada. He participates in the overthrow of the First Triumvirate.
    1813
    He is named Chief of the Northern Army.
    1816
    His daughter Mercedes Tomasa de San Martín y Escalada is born in Mendoza.
    1817
    On January 18, the Army of the Andes begins the crossing of the mountain range. On February 12 San Martin and his army, already in Chile, defeat the royalists in Chacabuco. Two days later San Martin enters Santiago. On March 18 he leaves for Buenos Aires in search of help to continue his campaign.
    1820
    On August 20 the liberating fleet set sail for Lima. On September 12 San Martin disembarked in the Peruvian port of Pisco.
    1821
    On July 12 he enters with his troops in Lima and proclaims the Independence of Peru. He is designated Protector of Peru. On August 28th he abolishes slavery and the mita and creates the Library of Peru.
    1830
    San Martin and his daughter settle in France
    1850
    On August 17, he died at his home.
    1880
    President Nicolás Avellaneda obtained the transfer of his remains to the Cathedral of Buenos Aires.
